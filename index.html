A Decade of Volatility (FY2015-FY2024)

The fiscal years 2015 through 2024 marked a period of significant economic turbulence for Pakistan. It saw phases of growth, often fueled by projects like CPEC, but these were frequently overshadowed by macroeconomic imbalances, external shocks like the COVID-19 pandemic (FY20) and devastating floods (FY23), leading to recurrent IMF interventions.

Real GDP Growth (Annual %)

Pakistan's GDP growth exhibited a 'boom-bust' cycle, with periods of acceleration followed by sharp slowdowns or contractions, highlighting a lack of sustainable, resilient growth.

Average CPI Inflation (Annual %)

Inflation remained relatively contained in the early part of the decade but surged dramatically from FY19 onwards, peaking in FY23, severely impacting living standards.

PKR/USD Exchange Rate (Avg. Fiscal Year)

The Pakistani Rupee saw significant depreciation against the US Dollar, particularly from FY18, increasing import costs and external debt servicing burdens.

Fiscal Deficit & Public Debt (% of GDP)

Persistent fiscal deficits, driven by low tax revenue and high expenditures, led to a stubbornly high public debt-to-GDP ratio, constraining fiscal space.

External Balances: Current Account & FX Reserves

The current account typically remained in deficit, often widening during growth periods. Foreign exchange reserves were volatile, frequently dropping to critically low levels, necessitating external support.

The "Genetic Code": Deep-Rooted Factors

Understanding Pakistan's recurring economic challenges requires looking at underlying structural issues that influence its growth potential and resilience.

üìâ Low Tax-to-GDP Ratio

Chronic inability to generate sufficient tax revenue (e.g., 11.1% in 2022). Narrow base, evasion, and weak enforcement are key issues. Reforms aim to broaden the base.

üí° Energy Sector & Circular Debt

Circular debt (over PKR 2.7T) is a major fiscal drain. Caused by IPP contracts, imported fuel reliance, T&D losses, poor recovery, and subsidies.

üè¢ SOE Performance

Inefficient State-Owned Enterprises incur losses, burdening the budget. Privatization/restructuring efforts are slow.

üö¢ Trade & Export Competitiveness

Struggles with a dynamic export sector. Overvalued exchange rates historically, protectionist policies, and concentrated export basket (textiles) are issues.

üèõÔ∏è Governance & Political Instability

Political volatility, policy discontinuity, lack of transparency, and corruption deter investment and hinder reforms.

ü§ù Reliance on External Financing (IMF)

Frequent recourse to IMF (over 20 times) due to chronic deficits and BoP crises. Conditionalities bring stabilization but also social costs and dependency concerns.
